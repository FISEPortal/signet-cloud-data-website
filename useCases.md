# Signet Use Cases

Signet addresses fundamental limitations of traditional cloud storage platforms — centralized control, data scanning and monetization, single points of failure, and lack of true ownership. By combining IPFS permanence, end-to-end encryption, NFT-proven ownership, and decentralized identity (DID), Signet gives users full sovereignty over their data.

---

## Personal Use Cases

### Secure, Private Backups of Sensitive Files

Store tax records, medical history, legal documents, passwords, or family heirlooms (scanned photos, letters) — encrypted on IPFS. No company can access them, and ownership is cryptographically proven via the NFT. Ideal for anyone who doesn't trust Big Tech or governments with their most sensitive data.

### Privacy-First Family & Friend Sharing

Share vacation photos, home videos, or financial spreadsheets with family members using time-limited decryption access. Recipients can view files only for the duration you allow — no permanent copy unless you explicitly permit it. No real identity required, just a decentralized ID.

### Digital Inheritance & Dead Man's Switch

Store wills, funeral instructions, crypto seed phrases, or final messages. Smart contract rules can grant temporary access to loved ones only after specified conditions are met — such as elapsed time or a trusted oracle confirming death — with no lawyer or bank required as intermediary.

### Personal Content Creation & Monetization

Independent creators — writers, photographers, musicians, 3D artists — upload original high-resolution files. The NFT proves ownership of the master. Sell temporary access directly to fans (e.g., "pay $5 to download raw photos for 30 days") without surrendering a large platform cut.

### Anonymous & Pseudonymous Collaboration

Students, researchers, and hobbyists can share notes, datasets, or project files with study groups or open-source collaborators without linking files to their real-world identity. Well-suited for privacy-conscious users or those operating in restrictive environments.

## Business & Professional Use Cases

The combination of IPFS permanence, encryption, NFT ownership, and sellable temporary access creates a new category: a decentralized, monetizable data layer that traditional SaaS cannot replicate.

### Confidential Document Sharing & Licensing

Companies store contracts, proprietary designs, source code, or financial models and grant temporary access to partners, clients, or auditors — without uploading to a central server that could be breached or subpoenaed. Access can be sold directly (e.g., "license this market research dataset for 90 days").

### Healthcare & Medical Records

Patients control their own encrypted health records and grant temporary decryption access to doctors, specialists, or insurers only when needed. The NFT proves record ownership, and decentralized IDs eliminate massive centralized databases that are vulnerable to breaches. Addresses HIPAA-style privacy requirements while enabling seamless, consent-driven sharing.

### Intellectual Property Protection & Monetization

Law firms, inventors, and studios store patents, screenplays, music masters, or game assets. The NFT acts as a tamper-proof certificate of authenticity. Access to the underlying files can be sold or licensed to buyers, making it easy to prove "this is the original" in disputes or commercial negotiations.

### Real Estate, Title & Escrow

Store deeds, surveys, inspection reports, and closing documents. Buyers and sellers use temporary access during transactions, and the NFT can represent the property title itself. Reduces reliance on title companies and accelerates cross-border transactions while maintaining privacy.

### Research Data & Academic Publishing

Scientists and universities store raw datasets, lab results, or papers and sell temporary access to other researchers or journals. Decentralized IDs allow collaborators to remain pseudonymous if desired. Data persists on IPFS permanently and cannot be altered or taken down.

### Whistleblower & Investigative Journalism

Journalists and insiders securely upload evidence or leaked documents, proving ownership via NFT while maintaining anonymity through decentralized IDs. Temporary access can be granted to newsrooms or fact-checkers without relying on centralized infrastructure that is vulnerable to takedown requests.

### Decentralized SaaS & dApp Backend

Other Web3 applications and startups can use Signet as the storage layer for user-generated content — a decentralized content marketplace, private messaging platform, or stock media library. Developers get native support for selling access to premium files or datasets.

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| **True data ownership** | The NFT + IPFS combination provides cryptographic proof of file ownership — enforceable in the marketplace or in court. |
| **Built-in monetization** | Access becomes a sellable product: pay-per-view, subscriptions, one-time licenses — all without a platform intermediary. |
| **Censorship resistance** | No single company or government can unilaterally delete or surveil stored files. |
| **No KYC friction** | Users onboard with a decentralized ID alone, dramatically lowering the barrier to entry. |

Signet is not simply another cloud drive. It is a **decentralized data economy layer** — combining a personal privacy vault, a business data licensing platform, and a Web3 content marketplace in a single system. The most compelling use cases will emerge from creators, privacy advocates, healthcare professionals, legal and tech firms, and anyone ready to own and monetize their data rather than surrender it for free.
